# Expedition Synthesis: B2B Compliance Site Visual Trust
## Date: 2026-03-25
## Vetted by: Orchestrator
## Alignment: Checked against Research Brief

### High Confidence (all teams + validators converged)
1. Stock lifestyle photos serve no trust function for compliance buyers and should be replaced
2. Statutory citation methodology is the site's strongest genuine trust signal — elevate it
3. Third-party reviews (Trustpilot/G2) are the largest addressable competitive gap
4. Content quality > visual polish for this buyer persona
5. Transparent pricing is a genuine strength — competitors hide theirs

### Synthesized Recommendation
Replace stock photos with documentary imagery (statute screenshots, document previews, state seals). Elevate methodology visibility. Add first-person voice to About page without full identity disclosure. Create Trustpilot profile but don't display widget until reviews accumulate. Add document sample preview to reduce no-refund anxiety.

### Disagreements
- Team 1 says stock removal is Priority 1. Team 3 says human identity absence is Priority 1. Validators sided with Team 3 — identity is more audience-specific.
- Priority sequence: (1) human voice on About, (2) replace stock with documentary images, (3) social proof infrastructure

### Filtered Out
- Cornell 2019 study: real but about secondhand marketplaces, not B2B compliance (V1 caught)
- "65% AI fingerprint" stat: fabricated precision (V1 caught)
- "38% leave due to generic design": broken citation chain (V1 caught)
- "97% check website first": unsourced editorial assertion (V1 caught)
- "Add team photos": violates safety constraint

### Risks
- Trustpilot widget with zero reviews is worse than no widget
- First-person voice without name still leaves accountability question partially open
- No direct A/B test evidence exists for this specific product category
