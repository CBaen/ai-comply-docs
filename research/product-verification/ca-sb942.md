## 2. Statute Citation

- **BPC § 22757** — Short title: "California AI Transparency Act"
- **BPC § 22757.1** — Definitions (covered provider, generative AI system, large online platform, provenance data, etc.)
- **BPC § 22757.2** — Core requirements: covered providers must maintain a free AI detection tool
- **BPC § 22757.3** — Licensing restrictions and requirements for third-party licensees
- **BPC § 22757.4** — Civil penalties and enforcement
- **BPC § 22757.5** — [Not individually fetched]
- **BPC § 22757.6** — Operative date provision

## 4. Effective Date

**Two dates apply:**

- **January 1, 2025:** The bill was signed September 19, 2024 and took effect January 1, 2025 as standard California statute (BPC § 22757 effective date noted as January 1, 2025).

- **August 2, 2026:** The chapter becomes **operative** on August 2, 2026. This means compliance obligations are not enforceable until August 2, 2026. (BPC § 22757.6: "This chapter shall become operative on August 2, 2026.")

The product data states "effective Aug 2, 2026" — this is the **operative date** (when compliance is required), not the technical effective date of the statute (January 1, 2025). The distinction matters for compliance purposes: businesses must have their AI detection tools ready by August 2, 2026.

AB 853 (Stats. 2025, Ch. 674) amended BPC § 22757.4, with an effective date of January 1, 2026 — this was a technical amendment to the penalty section, not a change to the operative date.

Confirmed from: BPC § 22757 (effective January 1, 2025), BPC § 22757.6 (operative August 2, 2026), BPC § 22757.4 (AB 853 amendment notation).

## 5. Penalty Amounts

**BPC § 22757.4(a)(1):** Civil penalty of **$5,000 per violation**.

**BPC § 22757.4(b):** Each day of continued violation is a separate discrete violation.

This means: a covered provider in continuous violation accrues $5,000 per day.

No penalty tiers found — the $5,000 is a flat per-violation amount, not a maximum. The statute says "shall be liable for a civil penalty in the amount of five thousand dollars ($5,000)" — this is a fixed amount, not "up to" $5,000.

**Attorney's fees:** Prevailing party in enforcement action is entitled to "all reasonable attorney's costs and fees" (BPC § 22757.4).

**For third-party licensee violations** (BPC § 22757.3(c)(3)): enforcement officials may pursue injunctive relief and reasonable attorney's fees and costs.

**The product data states "AG enforcement under Unfair Competition Law" as the enforcement mechanism.** This is incorrect — SB 942 has its own enforcement section (BPC § 22757.4) with its own penalty amounts. The UCL is not cited in the statute.

Confirmed from: BPC § 22757.4 full text, fetched from leginfo.legislature.ca.gov.

## 6. Enforcement

**Section 22757.4(a)(1):** Civil action may be brought by:
- California Attorney General
- City attorney
- County counsel

**No private right of action.** The statute limits enforcement to government officials.

**Method:** Civil action for penalties and injunctive relief.

The product data states "AG enforcement under Unfair Competition Law" — the correct statement is "AG, city attorney, or county counsel enforcement under BPC § 22757.4." The UCL is not the enforcement vehicle.

Confirmed from: BPC § 22757.4 full text, fetched from leginfo.legislature.ca.gov.

## 7. Cure Period

**None found in the statute.**

The statute contains one notable timeline: covered providers must revoke third-party licenses within 96 hours of discovering the licensee has disabled required disclosure capabilities (BPC § 22757.3(c)(2)). This is a compliance obligation, not a cure period before penalties apply.

No statutory cure period found. Confirmed from: BPC §§ 22757.2–22757.4 text review.

## 8. Applicability

**"Covered providers"** must comply with the core requirements.

**"Covered provider"** is defined in BPC § 22757.1 as:
"A person that creates, codes, or otherwise produces a generative artificial intelligence system that has over **1,000,000 monthly visitors or users** and is publicly accessible within the geographic boundaries of the state."

**"Generative AI system"** is defined as: A system that generates "derived synthetic content, including text, images, video, and audio" that emulates training data characteristics.

The law also applies to **"large online platforms"** (defined as public-facing platforms with 2,000,000+ unique monthly users, excluding broadband and telecom) with respect to certain disclosure requirements.

**Threshold:** 1,000,000 monthly users/visitors to the generative AI system. This is a significant threshold — small or niche AI tools are not covered.

Confirmed from: BPC § 22757.1 definitions page, fetched from leginfo.legislature.ca.gov.
