## 2. Statute Citation

AB 2013 adds **Title 15.2 (commencing with Section 3110) to Part 4 of Division 3 of the Civil Code**, creating:

- **Cal. Civ. Code § 3110** — Definitions (artificial intelligence, developer, generative AI, substantial modification, synthetic data generation, training)
- **Cal. Civ. Code § 3111** — Primary transparency requirement: developers must post training data documentation on their website before making generative AI systems publicly available to Californians

The product data citation "Cal. Civ. Code § 3273" is **incorrect**. The correct citation is **Cal. Civ. Code §§ 3110–3111**.

Note: Section 3111 was subsequently amended by AB 1170 (Stats. 2025, Ch. 67, Sec. 35), effective January 1, 2026. The amendment appears to be a technical/conforming amendment — the core requirements remain.

Confirmed from: leginfo.legislature.ca.gov bill page and CIV § 3110 text page.

## 4. Effective Date

**January 1, 2026.**

The bill requires compliance "on or before January 1, 2026, and before each time thereafter that a generative artificial intelligence system or service...is made publicly available to Californians."

Section 3111 was amended by AB 1170 (Stats. 2025, Ch. 67, Sec. 35), effective January 1, 2026. No amendment found that changed the compliance deadline.

**The "effective Jan 1, 2026" in the product data is correct.**

## 8. Applicability

**"Developers"** of generative AI systems made publicly available to Californians.

"Developer" is defined in CIV § 3110 as an entity that "designs, codes, produces, or substantially modifies" an AI system "for use by members of the public."

"Generative AI" is defined as a system that can "generate derived synthetic content, including text, images, video, and audio."

Three exemptions exist (CIV § 3111):
1. Systems used for security and integrity purposes
2. Aircraft operation
3. National defense applications

Confirmed from: CIV § 3110 definitions page fetched from leginfo.legislature.ca.gov.
