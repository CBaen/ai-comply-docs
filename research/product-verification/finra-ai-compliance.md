## Framing Note

This is NOT a standalone AI law. FINRA is the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority, a self-regulatory organization (SRO) that oversees broker-dealers. FINRA has not enacted AI-specific rules. Its existing rules apply to AI systems used by member firms. The compliance obligation comes from applying existing technology-neutral FINRA rules to AI use cases.

---

## 1. Enacted Status

FINRA rules are in effect. FINRA Regulatory Notice 24-09 (the primary AI-specific guidance document) was published June 27, 2024.

**No AI-specific FINRA rule has been enacted.** The notice explicitly states it "does not create new legal or regulatory requirements or new interpretations of existing requirements." The product's foundation is the application of existing FINRA rules to AI systems.

## 2. Rule/Guidance Citations

**Primary rules that apply to AI (per FINRA Regulatory Notice 24-09):**

- **FINRA Rule 3110 (Supervision):** Firms must maintain "a system to supervise the activities of each associated person that is reasonably designed to achieve compliance with applicable securities laws and regulations." When AI is used in supervisory systems, the system must address: technology governance, model risk management, data privacy and integrity, reliability and accuracy.

- **FINRA Rule 2210 (Communications with the Public):** Content standards (fair, balanced, not misleading) apply whether communications are generated by humans or AI. Suitability and disclosure requirements do not change because AI produced the output.

**Primary guidance document:**

- **FINRA Regulatory Notice 24-09:** "Regulatory Obligations When Using Generative Artificial Intelligence and Large Language Models" (June 27, 2024)

## 5. Penalty Amounts

**FINRA does NOT specify penalty amounts within rules or notices.** Enforcement actions and penalties are determined through FINRA's disciplinary process.

**General penalty authority:** FINRA's disciplinary process can impose fines, suspensions, bars, and disgorgement. Fine amounts in AI-related enforcement actions would be set by FINRA enforcement personnel based on the nature and seriousness of the violation.

**Context:** FINRA enforcement for Rule 3110 violations (inadequate supervision) has historically resulted in fines ranging from tens of thousands to millions of dollars depending on severity. For AI-specific violations, no public enforcement actions have been taken yet.

**CANNOT CONFIRM:** Any specific dollar penalty amounts for FINRA AI-related violations, because no AI-specific enforcement actions have been finalized with published penalties as of this verification.

---

## 6. Enforcement

**FINRA itself** enforces its rules through its Department of Enforcement. FINRA is a self-regulatory organization (SRO) — its authority derives from registration with the SEC under the Securities Exchange Act of 1934.

**SEC oversight:** The SEC has ultimate oversight authority over FINRA and can sanction FINRA for inadequate enforcement.

**No private right of action** under FINRA rules — individuals cannot sue directly for FINRA rule violations. Enforcement is through FINRA's disciplinary process or SEC oversight.

## 7. Applicability

**Who must comply:** FINRA member firms (registered broker-dealers). This includes:
- Investment banks
- Retail brokerages
- Clearing firms
- Independent broker-dealers

**Not covered:** Investment advisers (regulated by SEC under Investment Advisers Act), banks (regulated by federal banking regulators), hedge funds (generally not broker-dealers).

**Triggering AI use:** Any use of generative AI or LLMs in member firm operations — whether proprietary tools or third-party tools — falls under existing FINRA rules per Notice 24-09.

**Specific use cases covered:** AI in client communications, research output generation, supervisory systems, correspondence review, trading recommendations, compliance monitoring.

---

## 8. AI-Specific Provisions

FINRA has NO AI-specific rules. However, Notice 24-09 provides the following specific guidance:

**From Notice 24-09 (verified from primary source):**

1. **Pre-deployment evaluation:** Member firms must evaluate Gen AI tools BEFORE deployment to ensure continued compliance with existing FINRA rules.

2. **Supervision (Rule 3110):** Firms using AI in supervisory systems must address: (a) technology governance including model risk management, (b) data privacy and integrity, (c) reliability and accuracy of AI outputs.

3. **Communications (Rule 2210):** AI-generated content in client communications must meet the same "fair, balanced, and not misleading" standards as human-generated content. The rule applies regardless of whether AI produced the output.

4. **Technology-neutral application:** FINRA's rules "apply when member firms use AI...just as they apply when member firms use any other technology or tool."

5. **Third-party AI tools:** The same rules apply whether the firm builds its own AI or uses a vendor's AI system.

**What Notice 24-09 explicitly does NOT do:**
- Create new rules for AI
- Create new legal requirements
- Create new interpretations of existing requirements
