## CRITICAL FINDING: This Is a Privacy Law Amendment, Not a Standalone AI Regulation

Connecticut SB 1295 (Public Act 25-113) is **not** a standalone AI compliance law. It is an amendment to the Connecticut Data Privacy Act (CTDPA), Conn. Gen. Stat. §§ 42-515 et seq. It adds AI-specific disclosure requirements within the existing CTDPA framework.

**Practical implication for the product:** This law targets "controllers" subject to the CTDPA threshold (businesses that process personal data of 35,000+ Connecticut consumers, or that derive revenue from selling personal data). It does not create a general-purpose AI regulation applicable to all businesses. The product must accurately represent this scope — it is NOT analogous to the EU AI Act or TRAIGA.

**The product category may need reconsideration.** An "AI compliance" product for this law is really a "privacy notice compliance" product within the CTDPA framework.

## 5. Penalty Amounts

**PA 25-113 does not create new penalties.** Violations are enforced under the existing CTDPA penalty framework, which incorporates the Connecticut Unfair Trade Practices Act (CUTPA):

- Civil penalties **up to $5,000 per violation** (enforced via CUTPA, Conn. Gen. Stat. § 42-110b)
- Additional remedies available: injunctive relief, restitution, disgorgement of profits

**Section citation for penalties:** Conn. Gen. Stat. § 42-110b (CUTPA) — the CTDPA does not set its own penalty amount but routes enforcement through CUTPA. The CTDPA enforcement provision is in Conn. Gen. Stat. § 42-524 (the existing enforcement section).

**Important:** The $5,000 per violation figure is the CUTPA maximum that the AG can seek. This is not a mandatory amount — it is the maximum civil penalty per violation at AG discretion.

Source: Connecticut AG's official CTDPA page (https://portal.ct.gov/ag/sections/privacy/the-connecticut-data-privacy-act).

---

## 6. Enforcement

- **Enforcer:** Connecticut Attorney General, exclusively.
- **Mechanism:** Civil enforcement under CTDPA § 42-524, seeking civil penalties up to $5,000/violation under CUTPA, plus injunctive relief, restitution, disgorgement.
- **Private right of action:** None. The CTDPA explicitly excludes a private cause of action. The CT AG has exclusive enforcement authority.
- **Increased AG funding:** PA 25-113 reportedly increased funding for the AG's office to hire additional enforcement staff (per CT Senator Maroney).

Source: Connecticut AG's official CTDPA page; Inside Privacy analysis.

## 7. Cure Period

**The cure period that existed under the original CTDPA sunset on December 31, 2024.**

Under the original 2022 CTDPA, the AG was required to give controllers a 60-day right to cure before initiating enforcement. That right to cure expired December 31, 2024.

**PA 25-113 (SB 1295) does not appear to have reinstated a cure period** based on available secondary analysis. However, the full primary source text of PA 25-113 was not read (SSL error prevented automated fetch) and this could not be confirmed from primary source.

**This must be verified from the PA 25-113 primary text before launching the product.** See Section 10 for the browser prompt.

## 8. Applicability

**Who must comply:** Any "controller" subject to the CTDPA threshold:
- Conducts business in Connecticut or produces products/services targeted to Connecticut residents, AND
- Either: (a) processes personal data of 35,000+ Connecticut consumers during a calendar year, OR (b) processes personal data of 25,000+ consumers AND derives 25%+ of gross revenue from selling personal data.

**What triggers the AI obligation:** PA 25-113's LLM training disclosure applies when:
- The controller collects, uses, or sells personal data for the purpose of training large language models.
- Controllers meeting the CTDPA threshold must disclose this in their consumer-facing privacy notices.

**Profiling impact assessment obligation:** Triggered when a controller engages in profiling for purposes of making decisions with legal or similarly significant effects on consumers.

**This is NOT a regulation of frontier model developers or general-purpose AI providers.** It is a privacy notice amendment applicable to businesses that are already subject to the CTDPA and happen to use personal data for LLM training.
