## CRITICAL FINDING: Citation Mismatch — § 13-72 vs. § 13-70

The codebase citation is "Utah Code § 13-72." The introduced bill (SB0149) created sections numbered §§ 13-70-101 through 13-70-305 in the bill text. However, the **codified Utah Code chapter** is confirmed as **Chapter 72, Title 13** — titled the "Artificial Intelligence Policy Act," effective May 1, 2024.

**Resolution:** The chapter numbering changed during codification. The bill text used Chapter 70 as a placeholder; the enacted, codified law appears in Chapter 72. The citation "Utah Code § 13-72" is therefore correct for the codified statute.

**HOWEVER:** Chapter 72 (§§ 13-72-101 through 13-72-305) is ONLY the learning laboratory/regulatory sandbox portion of SB0149. SB0149 also created consumer-facing AI disclosure requirements in **§ 13-2-12** (Generative AI Liability section within the Division of Consumer Protection statutes) and amended **§ 13-11-4** (Utah Consumer Sales Practices Act). The $2,500 penalty and DCP enforcement cited in the codebase come from those sections, NOT from Chapter 72.

If the product is positioned as "Utah AI Policy Act compliance," the relevant citation for the product's actual compliance requirements (disclosure, penalties) should include §§ 13-2-12 and 13-11-4(2), not only § 13-72.

## 7. Applicability

**From the introduced bill (applicability of disclosure requirements):**

The disclosure requirements apply to persons who use generative AI in:
- Consumer protection-regulated activities (any business dealing with consumers)
- Regulated occupations (professions that are licensed or regulated by the state)

**Specific requirement:** Any person using generative AI to interact with consumers must disclose that they are using AI. Disclosure must be:
- Prominent (verbal at start of conversation; written notice before electronic exchanges)
- Before the AI provides any regulated occupational services

**"Generative AI" definition (from bill text):** Technology that uses machine learning and natural language models to generate outputs including text, images, or audio in response to prompts.

---

## 8. AI-Specific Provisions

This law is specifically about generative AI. Key provisions:

**Disclosure mandates:**
- Businesses using generative AI in consumer transactions must clearly notify consumers they are interacting with AI (not a human)
- Regulated occupations must disclose AI use before providing services
- Verbal disclosure required at start of any AI-enabled conversation
- Written disclosure required before written exchanges via AI

**Liability shield does not apply:**
- Use of generative AI is NOT a defense to consumer protection violations — if the AI makes a deceptive statement on behalf of a business, the business is liable

**Criminal liability:**
- § 76-2-107: Committing an offense "with aid of" generative AI can be an aggravating factor in criminal cases

**Regulatory sandbox (Chapter 72):**
- Companies can apply to participate in the AI learning laboratory
- Participants can receive temporary regulatory mitigation (including reduced civil fines and cure periods) for up to 12 months (extendable once)
- Administered by the Office of Artificial Intelligence Policy within state government
