## 5. Penalty Amounts

The ADMT regulations themselves do not create new penalties — they implement consumer rights under the CCPA. Violations of CPPA regulations (including ADMT regulations) are enforceable under the CCPA's existing penalty structure:

**Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.199.55 (administrative fines — CPPA enforcement):**
- Standard violations: up to **$2,500 per violation**
- Intentional violations or violations involving minors' personal information: up to **$7,500 per violation**

**Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.155 (same penalty structure — enforcement by AG):**
- Standard: up to $2,500 per violation
- Intentional/minors: up to $7,500 per violation

These amounts are subject to annual CPI adjustment per Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.199.95.

**The product data penalty amounts ($2,500/$7,500 per intentional violation) are correct**, with the note that standard (non-intentional) violations are capped at $2,500.

Confirmed from: CIV § 1798.199.55 and CIV § 1798.155 fetched from leginfo.legislature.ca.gov.

## 6. Enforcement

**Dual enforcement:**

1. **California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA):** Brings administrative enforcement actions, issues cease-and-desist orders, imposes administrative fines (CIV § 1798.199.55).

2. **California Attorney General:** Can bring civil enforcement actions for civil penalties (CIV § 1798.155).

**No private right of action** for ADMT regulation violations under the CCPA enforcement framework (the CCPA's limited private right of action is for data breaches only, not regulatory violations).

Confirmed from: CIV §§ 1798.155 and 1798.199.55 fetched from leginfo.legislature.ca.gov.

---

## 7. Cure Period

**The CCPA does not contain a cure period for CPPA administrative enforcement** of regulatory violations. The CPPA can issue enforcement orders after determining a violation.

Note: Prior to the CPRA amendments, the AG had a 30-day cure period provision. That provision expired January 1, 2023. No cure period exists under current law for CPPA administrative enforcement actions.

---

## 8. Applicability

The ADMT regulations apply to **businesses** subject to the CCPA that use automated decision-making technology in ways that affect consumers. The CCPA applies to for-profit businesses that:
- Have annual gross revenues exceeding $25 million; OR
- Annually buy, sell, or receive/share for commercial purposes personal information of 100,000+ consumers or households; OR
- Derive 50%+ of annual revenues from selling or sharing consumers' personal information

The ADMT-specific provisions implement consumer rights to:
- Access information about ADMT used on them
- Opt out of certain ADMT uses

The exact scope of the ADMT regulations (which decisions, which technologies, what opt-out mechanisms are required) was not fully extracted from the regulation text PDF.
