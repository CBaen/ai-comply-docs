# Keyword Strategy — Contestant 2

## Research Basis

All queries below are drawn from:
1. Live WebSearch on "Colorado SB 24-205 AI law compliance template small business 2026" — confirmed live buyer-intent SERP behavior
2. Google Search Console data in BRIEF.md — confirmed what queries the site already ranks for
3. Competitor SERP analysis — confirmed which queries law firms, platforms, and dedicated sites occupy
4. Applicability research ("do I need to comply") — confirmed the #1 pre-purchase anxiety

---

## The Core Insight: Buyer Queries Split Into Three Tiers

**Tier 1 — Deadline Anxiety ("I just found out"):**
`[state] AI law compliance deadline 2026`
`when does [law name] take effect`
`[state] AI law applies to small business`

**Tier 2 — Applicability Confirmation ("does this apply to me?"):**
`do I need to comply with [state] AI law`
`[law name] who is covered`
`[use case] + AI compliance + [state]`

**Tier 3 — Action Queries ("I need the documents"):**
`[state] AI law compliance template`
`[law name] compliance documents`
`[law name] documentation requirements`

The site currently ranks well for Tier 1 and Tier 2 queries but doesn't convert them because the product pages and blog posts are optimized as research resources, not buyer destinations.

The keyword strategy targets Tier 2 and Tier 3 where transactional intent is highest, while restructuring existing Tier 1 content to drive to product pages rather than to blog index.

## Three SEO Structural Recommendations

### 1. Stop sending buyer queries to blog posts

The Colorado blog post ranks for buyer queries but leads to a research article. Product pages should rank ABOVE blog posts for "-compliance documents" and "-template" query variants. Solution: add FAQ schema to product pages (see product-page-template.md), increase internal linking from blog to product, ensure product page `<title>` contains the exact buyer query phrase.

### 2. Create state-specific landing pages as product page entrances

Current: `/colorado-ai-compliance` exists as a landing page (appears in SERP). Good. This page should be structured as: 30 words on the law, 30 words on who it covers, then immediately → product card + buy button. Not 500 words of law summary. The law summary is already in the blog.

### 3. Fix the title tag hierarchy so product pages compete for transactional queries

Current product title: `Colorado SB 24-205 — AI Consumer Protections — Compliance Documents | AI Compliance Documents` — this is a subtitle-stacked pattern that dilutes keyword signal. Every "—" is a separate semantic unit that reduces the weight of each component. 

Proposed pattern for all products: `[Law Name] Compliance Documents — [Deadline or "In Force"] | Instant Download`

This pattern puts the transactional query term ("compliance documents") in the front half of the title, where Google weights it most heavily.
