# Ad Creative — Contestant 2

## Voice Applied to Ads

The new Transaction-First Declarative voice translates directly to ad creative: statute name in the headline, product noun in the description, price visible, zero abstract language. Google Ads in this voice are not awareness ads — they're interception ads. The buyer is already searching with a problem. The ad confirms: we have the solution, here's what it costs, click here.

## Reddit Native Ad

**Subreddit targets:** r/smallbusiness, r/Compliance, r/humanresources

**Title (≤60 chars):**
```
Your state's AI law applies to you. Here are the documents.
```
(58 chars ✓)

**Body (≤500 chars):**
```
If you use AI in hiring, lending, or customer decisions, state law probably requires documentation you don't have yet.

Colorado: deadline June 30, 2026. Texas: already in effect. Illinois: in effect since January 1. NYC: enforced since 2023.

These aren't enterprise-only laws. No size exemption. No revenue threshold.

We built the compliance documents from the enacted statute text. Not summaries. Not AI-generated overviews. The actual law.

$49–$697, instant download, one-time purchase.

aicompliancedocuments.com
```
(Character count: ~470 ✓)

**Image direction:**
Unsplash query: `small business owner laptop paperwork desk`
Filter: side-profile or over-shoulder, no smiling at camera, no visible tech products in foreground. Aspect ratio: 1200x628 (standard Reddit card).

**Why this tone for Reddit:** Reddit's small business communities have strong anti-ad reflexes. The native ad survives by being useful, specific, and honest — it names the laws, names the prices, and doesn't hide that it's an ad. No corporate hedging ("you may want to consider"), no urgency theater ("act before it's too late"), no false authority ("as recommended by leading compliance experts"). Just: here's the situation, here's the product, here's the price.

## Retargeting Concept

**Target:** Visitor who viewed `/products/colorado-sb24-205` and bounced without purchasing.

**Headline:**
```
Colorado's deadline is June 30. The documents are ready.
```

**Description:**
```
You looked at the Colorado SB 24-205 compliance package. It's 8 statute-sourced documents, $449, instant download. The deadline doesn't move.
```

**CTA:**
```
Get the Documents →
```

**Target URL:** `https://aicompliancedocuments.com/products/colorado-sb24-205`

**Retargeting rationale:** The visitor has already cleared the "does this apply to me?" barrier — they looked at the specific Colorado product page. The bounce is likely a price hesitation or a "I'll come back" decision. The retargeting ad does not re-explain the law. It names what they looked at (the package), reminds them of the price, and uses the deadline as the urgency signal. No discount offered — discounts undermine the "statute-sourced professional documents" positioning.

**Visual:** Static dark-navy card with white text. Law name, deadline, price. No photo. This card looks like a document — which is what they're buying.

## Imagery Direction Across All Ads

**The visual register for all ad creative:** operational, not aspirational.

Real people doing compliance-adjacent work — reviewing paperwork at a desk, sitting with a laptop in an office, working through documents in a small shop — not posed corporate photography. The buyer is a business owner, not a compliance officer. The image should look like their world, not the world they're trying to build into.

**What all ad imagery must avoid:**
- AI-related visuals (neural networks, robot hands, glowing circuits)
- Legal-profession clichés (gavels, scales of justice, law library shelves)
- Stock corporate diversity shots ("diverse team smiling in glass conference room")
- Technology lifestyle imagery (person looking at multiple screens in a dark office)
- Any image where the primary subject is looking at the camera and smiling

**Preferred Unsplash queries for ads:**
1. `small business owner laptop paperwork desk` — general state
2. `restaurant owner reviewing documents` — service industry use case
3. `small business HR manager office` — hiring use case
4. `retail shop owner working counter` — consumer-facing AI use case
5. `business person reading contract documents` — legal/compliance context

**Format rules:**
- Google Display: 1200x628. Dark-navy card with white text overlay is acceptable alternative to photo when the photo risks diluting the law-name readability.
- Reddit native: 1200x628 photo preferred (performs better than text cards in Reddit feed).
- Retargeting: Text card in navy/white. No photo — the buyer saw the product page; the message is the message.
