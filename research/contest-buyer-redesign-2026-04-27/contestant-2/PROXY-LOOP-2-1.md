## The coaching push

Item 1: Title tag for Colorado product page becomes "Colorado AI Law Compliance Package — SB 24-205 Deadline June 30, 2026 | AI Compliance Documents." Strong. A buyer searching "Colorado SB 24-205 compliance documents" sees a title that matches their query.

Item 2: The H1 becomes visible. The buyer who clicks through from that SERP title now lands on a page with a visible H1.

Here is where the journey needs scrutiny.

Your H1 for the *homepage* is "AI in your business. Five states have a law about that." That's a homepage H1 — for buyers who haven't navigated to a specific product yet. But item 1 optimized the *product page* title. When a buyer clicks the Colorado product page title in SERP, they land on the *product page*, not the homepage.

What is the product page H1 after items 1 and 2 ship? Your homepage-rewrite.md specs the homepage H1. Your product-page-template.md specs the Colorado product page. The product page H1 is "Colorado SB 24-205 Compliance Package" — unchanged from v1. That's fine. But now look at the journey:

SERP title: "Colorado AI Law Compliance Package — SB 24-205 Deadline June 30, 2026"
Product page H1: "Colorado SB 24-205 Compliance Package"

The buyer read "Deadline June 30, 2026" in the title. They clicked because of that urgency. They land on a product page H1 that names the law but doesn't repeat the deadline. The deadline appears in the hero deck below the H1: "June 30, 2026 is the deadline. These are the documents the law requires."

So the deadline is present — in the deck. But there's a half-second gap between the H1 (law name only) and the deck (deadline confirmed) where the buyer hasn't yet seen the urgency signal that brought them here. That half-second matters when the buyer arrived in a scan state.

The break is subtle but real: the SERP title created a deadline expectation. The product page H1 doesn't immediately fulfill that expectation. The deck does — but only after one more read.

This is the redesign question: after items 1 and 2 ship, does the product page hero visually confirm the deadline signal *at H1 level*, or does the buyer have to read one line further to find what they came for?

## A sharper version of items 1 and 2 together

Your voice spec is Transaction-First Declarative. The SERP title is declarative. The product page H1 is descriptive. They're not mismatched, but they're not in the same register.

What if the product page H1 carried the deadline explicitly? Something like: "Colorado SB 24-205 — June 30, 2026." Law name and date, nothing else. The deadline that brought them there is confirmed at the first element they read.

That's one word change. It costs nothing. And it closes the gap between what SERP promised and what the page delivers in the first half-second.

Your Build Order is right. Items 1 and 2 first — absolutely. But before those ship, check whether the product page H1 needs a one-word adjustment to honor the promise the title tag makes. That's not a new item. It's the completion of item 1.
