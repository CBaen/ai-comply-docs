## Positioning Statement

**AI Compliance Documents** sells the documents that state AI laws require. Five states have AI compliance laws in effect or effective by June 30, 2026. Those laws name specific documents: risk management policies, impact assessments, consumer notices, bias audits. If you deploy AI in employment, lending, insurance, or consumer decisions in Colorado, Texas, Illinois, New York City, or California, you owe those documents — whether or not you've heard of the law. This is not a compliance platform. This is not legal advice. This is a document store: $49–$697, one-time purchase, instant download, built from the enacted statute text, ready for your attorney to review.

## Voice Change Note (v1 → v2)

v1 positioning used Realist/Credentialist pattern: buyer journey framing, "the person who just found out this is their job," explanatory. Correct voice for blog posts. Wrong for a positioning statement — explains who benefits instead of stating what's for sale.

v2 uses Transaction-First Declarative: names the product ("document store"), names the laws, names the price, states the obligation as fact, clarifies what the product is not. No buyer journey narrated. The buyer reads this and recognizes themselves — or they don't, and they're not the buyer.

## Rationale

### Who it names explicitly

"Small or mid-sized business owner who just found out they're using AI in employment, lending, insurance, or consumer decisions."

This is not abstract. It mirrors the exact triggering event the brief describes: counsel flagged an issue, a vendor due diligence form surfaced a gap, or news anxiety pushed someone to search. The buyer is not browsing. They arrived under duress.

### Why-now anchor

Three concrete deadlines: Colorado June 30, Texas January 1 (already passed), NYC active since 2023, Illinois January 1 (already active). The "why now" is not manufactured — it's baked into the statute dates.

### What it is NOT

Explicitly names two things it's not competing with: platforms (OneTrust-style ongoing subscriptions) and law firms (hourly advice). This clears the price confusion immediately. The buyer does not know what category this product is. The positioning statement tells them: templates. One time. For attorney review.

### Differentiation claim

"Every number in every document traces back to the enacted law text." This is not aspirational — it's the site's documented methodology. It is also the single hardest thing for competitors (law firm blog posts, generic compliance platforms) to replicate. Most competitors synthesize. This site sources.

### Anti-default check

- No "discover/unlock/transform" verbs — none used
- No "trusted by X businesses" — omitted
- No generic bullets — this is a single paragraph
- No time estimate language — says "before Colorado's June 30 deadline," not "in time for the deadline"
