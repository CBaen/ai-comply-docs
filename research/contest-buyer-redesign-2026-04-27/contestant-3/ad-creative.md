# Ad Creative — Contestant 3

## Voice Anchor for Ads

All ad copy follows the new marketing voice: short, declarative, product-forward, penalty-exact. No "discover," no "trusted by," no AI-framing, no fabricated stats. Every penalty figure traces to a verified primary source (Illinois penalties: 775 ILCS 5/8A-104, fetched live; Colorado: Colorado Consumer Protection Act; NYC: DCWP enforcement data). Ads name the law, name the product, name the price. That's it.

## Reddit Native Ad

**Target subreddit:** r/smallbusiness (primary) / r/humanresources (secondary)
**Format:** Native — should read as a post, not an ad. No "sponsored" framing in the body. Honest, direct, non-salesy.

**Title (≤60 chars):**
```
Does your state's AI hiring law apply to you?
```

**Body (≤500 chars):**
```
If you use any software that screens, scores, or ranks job applicants, you might be covered by Colorado's AI law (June 30 deadline), Illinois HB3773 (already in effect), or NYC Local Law 144 (been enforcing since 2023).

These laws require specific documentation — not just a policy, but specific notices, impact assessments, and audit templates.

We built those documents from the actual statute text. Instant download, $49–$697 depending on your state.

Not legal advice. Just the documents the law describes.

aicompliancedocuments.com
```

**Image direction:** Unsplash — `"HR manager laptop desk serious"`. A single person at a desk looking at a screen, natural light. No stock-corporate imagery. Image should make the viewer think "that could be me" — not "that's a compliance professional." Aspect ratio: 16:9, landscape, for Reddit's native image format.

**Why this works for Reddit:** Reddit buyers hate ads that read like ads. The title is a question they're already asking themselves. The body is honest about what the product is (documents, not advice) and what it isn't (not a full audit service, not a law firm). The closing line "Not legal advice. Just the documents the law describes." pre-empts the most common Reddit objection and models the site's actual positioning.

## Retargeting Concept

**Target:** Someone who visited a product page (e.g., `/products/colorado-sb24-205`) and bounced without purchasing.
**Trigger:** Visited for >30 seconds, did not initiate questionnaire.
**Format:** Google Display Network or Meta retargeting banner. Text-primary, not image-primary.

**Headline:**
```
You looked at the Colorado compliance documents.
```

**Description:**
```
June 30 is the deadline. 8 documents, $449, instant download. Everything the statute requires — built from C.R.S. § 6-1-1702.
```

**CTA:**
```
Complete Your Purchase
```

**Why this headline works:** It acknowledges the prior visit without pretending it didn't happen. It names the specific product and the specific deadline. It doesn't try to re-explain what the site is — the person already knows. The only new information is the deadline reiteration. The CTA "Complete Your Purchase" implies the buyer was already in process, which reduces psychological distance to conversion.

**Image direction for retargeting:** Plain white or very light gray background. Text-dominant. If imagery is needed: `"blank document desk professional"` — emphasizes the "you don't have this yet" gap.

## Imagery Direction Across All Ads

One paragraph for all ad imagery:

The ads should look like they come from a company that understands the buyer's actual situation — a real person in a real business who has a compliance problem. No AI-glow, no courthouse imagery, no abstract data visualization. Every image should make the buyer think "that's me" or "that's my situation." Images with natural light, real offices, single people (not teams), and visible work context (papers, laptops, phone calls) perform best with this buyer. No posed group shots. No aspirational lifestyle. The buyer is anxious, not aspirational — the imagery should feel like it recognizes that anxiety, not sells past it.

When in doubt: one person, one desk, natural light, realistic expression. That's the register.
