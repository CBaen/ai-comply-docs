# Homepage Rewrite — Contestant 3

## Current (to beat)

- `<title>`: "AI Compliance Documents — Templates for Every State AI Law"
- `<meta description>`: "AI compliance templates for 14+ state laws, the EU AI Act, and federal frameworks. Built from enacted statute text. Instant download."
- H1 (screen-reader only): "AI Compliance Documents — State AI Compliance Templates"
- No visible above-fold copy stating who this is for or what deadline is active

**Problem:** The title names the catalog, not the buyer's problem. The meta description leads with breadth ("14+ state laws") when the buyer is searching for their specific state's law. The hero section has no visible H1 — it's screen-reader only and renders a product carousel instead of a buyer-oriented headline. A business owner who lands here doesn't know in the first 10 seconds whether this site is for them.

---

## Rewrite

### `<title>` tag

```
AI Compliance Documents — Colorado, NYC, Illinois AI Law Templates
```

**Rationale:** Replaces "Templates for Every State AI Law" (catalog framing) with the three highest-urgency state laws by name. Buyers searching "[state] AI compliance template" will see their state in the SERP title, dramatically increasing click relevance. Stays within 60-char limit for desktop display. The three states named cover the highest-traffic buyer queries confirmed in research.

**Alternative for A/B testing:**
```
Colorado AI Law Compliance Documents — June 30, 2026 Deadline
```
(Runs on the Colorado product page title; the homepage title should remain multi-state.)

### `<meta description>`

```
Colorado's AI law deadline is June 30, 2026. NYC Local Law 144 is in force now. Illinois penalties reach $70,000 per violation. Get the documents your law requires — built from enacted statute text, instant download, $49–$697.
```

**Rationale:** Opens with the three urgency facts a buyer actually needs to hear. Each sentence answers a buyer question: "Does my state have a deadline?" / "Is this already active?" / "What's my risk?" Closes with the product value proposition (documents, statute-built, instant, priced). 158 characters — within 160-char display limit. Every competitor's meta description says "compliance solution" or "framework" — ours is the only one that says "June 30, 2026."

### H1 (visible, above fold)

```
Your state passed an AI law. Here's what you need to comply.
```

**Rationale:** Written in second person, present tense. Does not say "discover," "unlock," or "transform." States the buyer's situation ("your state passed an AI law") and immediately answers it ("here's what you need"). 60 characters — punchy, mobile-legible. This is the Pragmatic Realist voice opening: direct address, no qualifier, reader knows where they stand in the first sentence.

---

### Sub-H1 / Lede paragraph (below H1, above CTA)

```
Colorado, Illinois, New York City, California, and Texas have each enacted AI laws covering hiring, lending, insurance, healthcare, and consumer decisions. Penalties run from $500 per day (NYC) to $70,000 per violation (Illinois). If you use AI in those decisions and haven't documented your compliance, you have a gap the law doesn't forgive.
```

**Rationale:** Names five states (covers the top five buyer-intent query clusters confirmed in research). States penalty amounts from verified primary sources (Illinois: 775 ILCS 5/8A-104, fetched live; NYC: DCWP enforcement data). Ends with the consequence frame that converts researchers to buyers: "a gap the law doesn't forgive." No soft language ("could face fines") — statute-exact language per the site's own editorial rules.

---

### Primary CTA

```
Find My Compliance Package
```

**Rationale:** Replaces "Browse Products" (catalog language) with buyer-oriented action. The word "my" signals that the site will direct them to what they specifically need, not show them a menu. The CTA resolves the pre-purchase objection surfaced in SMB research: "I don't know which law applies to me." This CTA promises to answer that question.

**CTA sub-copy (displayed below button):**
```
Colorado · Illinois · NYC · California · Texas — pick your state, download your documents
```

**Rationale:** Answers the implicit question "does this cover my state?" in the same visual element as the CTA. State names act as scent markers for the buyer's specific search query.

### Pain Section headline revision

```
Current: "What happens if you don't comply?"
Revised: "The AG doesn't need to find you proactively. One complaint does it."
```

**Rationale:** "What happens if you don't comply?" is a generic headline that every competitor uses. The revised version names the actual mechanism: a complaint triggers an investigation. This is the single most underestimated compliance risk for SMBs — they assume enforcement is proactive and rare. The revised headline is the Pragmatic Realist voice naming the reader's actual misconception before they articulate it.

### Final CTA Section (bottom of page)

```
Current H2: "Don't wait for a complaint"
Revised H2: "Colorado's deadline is June 30. NYC is enforcing now. Illinois penalties start at $16,000."
```

```
Current sub-copy: "AI regulations are in effect now. Get your compliance documents today for a fraction of what a law firm charges."
Revised sub-copy: "You don't need to know everything about the law. You need the specific documents it requires. Pick your state, answer ten questions, download your package. That's it."
```

**Rationale:** The current bottom-of-page CTA repeats the same "don't wait" framing as the hero without adding new information. The revised version names three specific facts (deadline, enforcement status, penalty floor — all verified from primary sources) that the buyer may have missed on the way down the page. The sub-copy answers the most common SMB objection: "I'm not a compliance expert, I can't assess this." The revised copy lowers the barrier: you don't need expertise, just ten minutes.
