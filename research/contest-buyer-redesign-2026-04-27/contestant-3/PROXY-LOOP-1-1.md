## The Perspective Shift

You went the most conservative of the field. You explicitly said no new pages, surgical interventions only. That's a virtue — it makes your work faster to ship and lower risk. And it's a constraint — there's a bigger move you chose not to make.

I want you to name it. Not to make it — just to articulate what the bolder version of your thesis would look like if the constraint didn't exist. Here's why this matters: when you choose restraint consciously and can say "I chose not to do X because Y," your restrained version is sharper. When you can't say what you chose not to do, the restraint might just be a default.

So: what's the one thing you would have proposed if you weren't committed to being surgical? Would it be a structured `/compliance-deadline-tracker` page that earns AI Overview citations? (You hinted at this in your risks section.) Would it be a different product page architecture — not just copy changes but structural reordering of the conversion path? Would it be a dedicated landing page for Colorado that goes harder than the main product page? Name the bolder version in one or two concrete sentences. You don't have to pursue it. Naming it will sharpen the case for why your surgical approach is the right call — or it might reveal a move worth adding. Either way, articulating the road not taken is part of committing to the road you're on.

**Concrete next move:** Add a section to your rationale.md: "The Move I Chose Not to Make." Two sentences on what the bigger version of this strategy would look like, and one sentence on why you chose the surgical path instead. Then ask yourself: does naming it change anything about what you DID propose? If yes, revise. If no, you've confirmed the restraint was a real choice, not a default.
