## Core Positioning (new voice)

Your state has an AI law. It requires specific documents. We built them.

That's the positioning. The buyer isn't looking for a compliance framework or a research resource. They're looking for the thing the law actually requires — a notice, an impact assessment, a risk management policy — and they want it today. AI Compliance Documents is the only place that sells exactly those documents, built from the enacted statute text, at a price a small business can pay, with a download button instead of a sales call.

Colorado SB 24-205: deadline June 30, 2026. Eight documents. $449.
Illinois HB3773: in effect now. Penalties up to $70,000 per violation (775 ILCS 5/8A-104). Five documents. $397.
NYC Local Law 144: enforcing since 2023. Seven documents. $399.

The site is a compliance store. Not a research library. Not a SaaS platform. Not a law firm. The documents the law names — those documents, built from the statute, instant download, one-time purchase.

---

## Who This Is For

Small and mid-size business owners who use AI in hiring, lending, insurance, healthcare, or consumer decisions. The HR director who just got flagged by outside counsel. The staffing agency whose ATS vendor said Local Law 144 applies to them. The restaurant owner who read about Colorado's deadline and didn't know where to start. The person who just found out this is their job.

## Who This Is Not For

Researchers. Consultants pulling free frameworks. Enterprise compliance departments. Law firms shopping for templates to rebrand.

---

## What Doesn't Change

Statute precision. Every penalty figure, every citation, every effective date traces to a .gov primary source fetched in this session. Confidence in the voice does not mean looseness in the facts. The Precise Credentialist moves to where it belongs — inside the product and the blog, not in the hero copy. The pricing ($49–$697) stays. The one-time purchase model stays. The "not legal advice" framing stays.

---

## Competitive Position (plain statement)

- TrustArc / OneTrust: enterprise subscriptions, require sales calls, no instant download. We are the opposite.
- Law firms: $400–$800/hour, weeks to draft. We are the opposite.
- Doing nothing: one employee complaint triggers an AG investigation. The first thing the state asks for is your documentation. We sell that documentation.
