## Unpacked: Who, What, Why Now

**Who:** Small and mid-size business owners — not compliance professionals, not legal departments, not researchers. The HR director who just got flagged by outside counsel. The restaurant owner whose ATS vendor told them Local Law 144 applies. The staffing firm that got a cease-and-desist inquiry and needs documentation fast. The person who just found out this is their problem.

**What:** Instant-download, statute-built PDF compliance document packages. Not advice. Not a platform subscription. Not a certification. The specific documents the law requires: employee notices, impact assessments, risk management policies, bias audit summary templates — all drafted against the enacted statute text.

**Why now:** Every buyer is in the site's catalog because a deadline exists or enforcement is active. Colorado's deadline is June 30, 2026 — the most urgent active AI law in the country for businesses using AI in consequential decisions. NYC Local Law 144 has been enforcing since 2023, with DCWP investigations increasing in 2026. Illinois HB3773 is in effect with $70,000-per-violation penalty exposure. These are not hypothetical risks. The buyer who finds this site after getting a legal flag needs documentation in hours, not weeks.

**Positioning against competitors:**
- Against TrustArc/OneTrust: They require enterprise sales calls and sell subscriptions. We sell the documents, instantly, for under $700.
- Against law firms: They charge $400-$800/hour and take weeks to draft what we deliver in ten minutes.
- Against doing nothing: The AG doesn't need to find you proactively. A single employee complaint triggers an investigation. The first thing the state asks for is your documentation.
