# Loop 2 Applied — Contestant 3

## What Changed

Two targeted edits. First, `product-page-template.md` Section 1 now contains the recognition sentence the Proxy asked for: "If you also built or substantially modified the AI tool you're deploying, the statute assigns you separate developer obligations on top of these — including disclosure requirements to your own deployers. These 8 documents cover the deployer side." This sentence does what the Proxy named — it surfaces the deployer/developer distinction without turning it into an explainer. Most buyers on the Colorado page are deployers who didn't build their tool. The sentence speaks to the exception (the buyer who is also a developer) without redirecting the page toward that exception. The buyer who needs it will stop. The buyer who doesn't will scroll. The sentence demonstrates the site read the statute without announcing that it did. Second, `voice-spec.md` gains a "Recognition Principle" section that makes this pattern explicit as a voice rule — one statute-sourced recognition sentence per product page, delivered in the marketing voice, not as explanation — so any writer applying the spec knows to look for the one thing the buyer didn't know to ask. The section includes example recognition sentences for Illinois (zip-code-as-proxy explicitly prohibited by name in the statute) and NYC (bias audit must be conducted by an independent auditor — the documents are the pre-audit infrastructure, not the audit itself).

## What I Held

The semantic color system stays as-is. The Proxy noted correctly that the buyer can't see the color rule directly — they see the result. That's fine. The result (amber means urgency and only urgency; green means statute-verified and only that) is what matters to the buyer. The system doesn't need to be explained to be effective. Restraint in the color rules is the expertise made visible through outcome, not through declaration. I didn't add a "why our colors mean something" section — that would be the thing the hardware store person never says.
