## Reddit Native Ad

**Platform:** r/smallbusiness (primary) / r/Compliance (secondary)
**Format:** Native — reads like an experienced business owner sharing a resource, not an ad for an enterprise platform.

**Title (≤60 chars):**
`State AI laws are in effect. Here's where to get the docs.`
(58 chars — within limit)

**Body (≤500 chars):**
> Illinois HB3773 has been in effect since January 1, 2026. NYC Local Law 144 since July 2023. Colorado SB 24-205 is 64 days out (June 30). Texas TRAIGA since January.
>
> If you use any AI in hiring, lending, or consumer decisions — you probably need compliance documents.
>
> We built statute-sourced templates for all of them. Not summaries. Not frameworks. The actual documents the law requires: employee notices, impact assessments, risk management policies. From $49, instant download.
>
> aicompliancedocuments.com — no subscription, no enterprise pricing, no demo call.

(467 chars — within limit)

**Why this works on Reddit:** Reddit r/smallbusiness readers are hostile to advertising that sounds like advertising. This copy:
- Opens with facts (specific laws, specific dates) — not with a pitch
- Names the problem before the solution
- Distinguishes itself from enterprise platforms without disparaging them
- Ends with what it is NOT (subscription, enterprise, demo) — which is the primary objection a Reddit reader would have to a compliance SaaS ad

**Image direction for Reddit native:**
Unsplash query: `"small business owner paperwork desk"` — a real person at a real desk with actual documents. Not a laptop-with-city-view, not a handshake, not a gavel. The image should make the buyer think "that's me" — which is the native ad's job.

## Retargeting Concept

**Target:** Visitor who viewed `/products/colorado-sb24-205` and did not purchase. Session ended without clicking "Customize Now" or "Quick Purchase."

**Platform:** Google Display / Meta (audience: site retargeting, 7-day window)

**Headline:**
> You looked at the Colorado compliance package.

**Sub-headline:**
> June 30, 2026 is still the deadline.

**Description:**
> Colorado SB 24-205 requires a risk management program, impact assessment, and consumer notices. If you weren't ready to purchase then — the package is still here. $449, instant download.

**CTA button:**
> Get My Colorado Documents →

**What this does NOT do:**
- No urgency fake-out ("Only 3 left!" — there is no inventory)
- No discount offer (all sales are final, no price change warranted)
- No new information — just restates what the buyer already saw, gently, factually

**Why "You looked at" is the right opener:** It's honest about the retargeting context. Pretending the ad isn't a retargeting ad ("Still thinking about AI compliance?") is less trustworthy than acknowledging the prior visit directly. The buyer who was deadline-anxious when they first visited is still deadline-anxious — they just needed a push to come back.

## Imagery Direction — Across All Ads

One paragraph, applying to all four ad formats:

The visual register across all ad creative is **documentary, not aspirational.** Real business environments — offices with actual clutter, small teams, physical paperwork visible. No stock-corporate visual tropes: no smiling people in suits pointing at whiteboards, no AI-glow computer screens, no abstract legal symbolism (gavels, scales). No imagery that reads as "large enterprise" — the buyer is a small business owner or HR director, not a Fortune 500 compliance department. Unsplash queries that work: `"small business paperwork"`, `"HR director documents"`, `"small office team"`, `"business owner laptop documents"`. Filter for natural light, visible documents, and people looking at their work rather than at the camera. The visual signal is "your world, organized" — not "aspirational future state."
