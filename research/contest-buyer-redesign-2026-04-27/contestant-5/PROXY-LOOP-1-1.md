## The Perspective Shift

Your strategy says the EEOC blog post's 0.11% CTR is a title-and-meta problem. So let's be concrete about that specific post, right now. Walk it through your own redesign.

The EEOC blog post is at 1,833 impressions, position 4.16, 0.11% CTR. Its current title is something like "EEOC AI Guidance Removed: The Federal Vacuum and What It Means for 2026" — an informational news headline. After your changes ship:

What is the new title? Your strategy pivots product page titles toward deadline urgency and buyer language. But the EEOC post is a blog post, not a product page. Does your blog CTA pattern change the SERP representation of that post at all? Does Google re-index a blog post's title/meta when you inject a CTA component into the body? (No — a body CTA doesn't change the title tag or meta description.) So if your strategy fixes product page titles and blog body CTAs, what specifically happens to this post's 0.11% CTR?

This is not a trick question — it may be that the EEOC post's CTR can't be fixed by your strategy, because the zero-click pressure is structural (AI Overviews consuming the answer), not navigational. If that's true, say it explicitly. But then: what does your redesign do for the traffic that DOES click through to that post? Does the blog CTA pattern catch that buyer? Where in the post does the CTA appear, and what does it say?

**Concrete next moves:** Write one concrete "after-state" for the EEOC blog post under your redesign. What is the new title (if you're proposing one)? What does the SERP listing look like after your changes? What happens to the visitor who clicks through? This exercise will either surface a gap in your strategy (blog titles aren't addressed) or confirm that your strategy correctly deprioritizes unfixable zero-click posts in favor of fixable product pages. Either conclusion sharpens the work.
