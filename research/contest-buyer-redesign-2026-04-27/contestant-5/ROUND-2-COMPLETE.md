# ROUND-2-COMPLETE — Contestant 5

## Concept summary

Round 2 applied the new Calm Hardware Store voice and the full brand overhaul GL asked for — visual system, IA, ad creative, and rewritten marketing surfaces — on top of the Round 1 diagnostic foundation.

The core diagnostic frame held: 0.11% CTR at position 4.16 is a representation problem, not a ranking problem. Round 2 extends that diagnosis from SERP snippets to every buyer-facing surface. The site currently looks like an information resource at every level — title tags, hero copy, section order, nav labels, product cards, blog CTAs. Each deliverable in Round 2 attacks one layer of that pattern.

**The five moves that define this entry:**

1. **New voice: Calm Hardware Store.** Short, declarative, offer-forward. "Your state has an AI law. We built the documents that comply with it." No explanation. No adjectives. The offer speaks. The blog body stays Realist/Credentialist — the voice change is surgical to marketing surfaces only.

2. **Visual system that signals solutions provider, not editorial resource.** Slate Navy + Signal Blue + Deadline Red palette. No carousel. No hero landscape photo. Static product grid ordered by enforcement urgency. Product cards lead with status badge and deadline date in red — the buyer sees urgency before they read a word.

3. **IA overhaul: Products front, Blog demoted.** Nav becomes "Find Your Documents / States / Resources / FAQ / About." Homepage collapses from 10 sections to 5. Product grid moves to section 2 (currently section 3 inside the hero). Penalty section moves to section 3 (currently section 7). The buyer gets the offer, the stakes, and the trust signal in one scroll.

4. **Blog title/meta rewrites that shift SERP targeting from informational to buyer-intent.** The Proxy's gap from Loop 1 is addressed: the EEOC post gets a new title ("EEOC Removed AI Hiring Guidance. Illinois, NYC, and Colorado Filled the Gap.") and meta that ends with an offer, not a description. Every high-traffic post gets a title that names who is affected and points toward resolution, not just what the law says.

5. **Ad creative in the new voice.** Three RSAs (Colorado / Illinois / NYC), one Reddit native that reads like a peer recommendation not a platform ad, one retargeting concept that is honest about the retargeting context. All penalty amounts statute-verified. No fabricated stats.

Every penalty amount, effective date, and statute citation traces to a verified primary source established in Round 1 research. The voice changed. The integrity didn't.
