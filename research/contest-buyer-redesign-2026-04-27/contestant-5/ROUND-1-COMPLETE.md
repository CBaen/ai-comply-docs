# ROUND-1-COMPLETE — Contestant 5

## Concept summary

The core diagnosis: 10,513 impressions and 0 sales is not a traffic problem — it is a representation problem at the SERP level and a conversion problem at the page level. The site's titles and meta descriptions are catalog-coded (researcher bait), and even when a buyer lands, the page order buries the stakes (penalty amounts) behind the features (document list). This redesign fixes both without changing a line of the site's actual content quality.

**The three moves:**

1. **SERP — title/meta pivot from catalog to urgency.** "AI Compliance Documents — Templates for Every State AI Law" → "Colorado AI Law Deadline: June 30, 2026 — Compliance Documents for Small Business." The buyer searching with a deadline in mind clicks the title that names their deadline. Position 4.16 with 0.11% CTR is a title problem, not a ranking problem.

2. **Product page — penalties move before documents.** The buyer's motivation is the stakes (up to $20,000 per consumer under C.R.S. § 6-1-112(1)(a) for Colorado; up to $70,000 per violation for Illinois; up to $200,000 for Texas). Those numbers need to appear in section 2, not section 7. The affirmative defense language ("these documents are your affirmative defense documentation") is the conversion bridge that connects the stakes directly to the product.

3. **Blog — three product links per post, placed at conversion moments.** The blog posts are information-complete but conversion-leaky. The fix is not shorter posts — it is product links at the penalty section, the requirement list, and the closing action section. 1 link per 1,500 words → minimum 3 links per post, all contextual, all at points of maximum buyer readiness.

One optional new page: `/compliance-deadline-by-state` — a buyer-routing page for the buyer who knows they have a problem but doesn't yet know which specific law applies to them.

All penalty amounts, effective dates, and statute citations verified against primary sources fetched this session.
