# Positioning Statement — Wildcard Synthesis

## Core Positioning

Your state has an AI law. It requires specific documents. We built them.

That's the whole thing. The buyer isn't looking for a compliance framework, a research library, or a legal opinion. They're looking for the thing the law actually names — a risk management policy, an impact assessment, a consumer notice — and they want it today. AI Compliance Documents is the only place that sells exactly those documents, built from the enacted statute text, at a price a small business can pay, as an instant download.

Two modes. Same product. Different urgency.

If you operate in Illinois, NYC, or Texas: the deadline has passed. You are currently exposed. Documentation is your first defense and the cheapest one available. The Attorney General doesn't need to find you proactively — one employee complaint triggers an investigation, and the first question is "where is your documentation?"

If you operate in Colorado: the deadline is June 30, 2026. You have a window. Use it. $449 for the 8 documents SB 24-205 requires of deployers — built from C.R.S. § 6-1-1701, not from a law firm's summary.

Not a platform. Not a subscription. Not a $400/hour attorney. The documents — at a price your business can pay — in the format the law requires — available right now.

## Voice Note

The positioning lives at the intersection of two registers: the Calm Hardware Store voice for marketing surfaces (short, declarative, offer-forward) and the Precise Credentialist voice for the statute-exact facts that make the positioning credible. These two registers don't compete — the hardware store voice says "we have what you need," and the credentialist facts say "here's how we know that's true." Neither inflates. Neither hedges.

The buyer who reads this positioning should know three things in 10 seconds: their law is real, the documents exist, and the price is visible. Everything else is detail.
