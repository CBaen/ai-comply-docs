# Ad Creative — Wildcard Synthesis

## Voice Standard

All ad creative uses the Calm Hardware Store voice in Deadline Approaching or Already Exposed mode. No discovery verbs. No "trusted by N businesses." Law names, penalty figures, and prices are statute-verified (or flagged for verification). The two-mode frame applies to ad copy the same way it applies to product pages: deadline-facing copy for Colorado, enforcement-present copy for Illinois/NYC/Texas.

## Reddit Native Ad

**Target:** r/smallbusiness or r/Compliance

**Title (≤60 chars):**
```
Colorado AI law: deadline June 30. Does it apply to you?
```

**Body (≤500 chars, native-feel — not display-feel):**
```
A lot of small business owners I've talked to don't know Colorado's AI law (SB 24-205) takes effect June 30, 2026. If you use AI in hiring, lending, or consumer decisions in Colorado, you're a deployer — and you need specific documentation.

We built the 8 documents the law requires from the actual statute text. Risk management policy, impact assessment, consumer notices. $449, instant download. Not a law firm. Not a platform subscription.

aicompliancedocuments.com — compliance-deadline-by-state if you're not sure which law applies to you.
```

**Image direction:** Unsplash search: "small business owner laptop desk papers" — a real person in a real work context, not a stock-corporate model. No AI imagery, no legal symbolism.

**Voice note:** Reddit native ads read as peer-to-peer, not corporate. The opener ("A lot of small business owners I've talked to...") signals this person has experience with the buyer's situation, not a brand talking at them. The product is named plainly, the price is visible, and the disclaimer ("not a law firm") handles the first objection in-copy. The sub-CTA ("/compliance-deadline-by-state if you're not sure which law applies") gives hesitant readers a lower-friction entry point.


**CTA:**
```

```

**Image direction:** Deadline Amber color fill with white text. No photography — the deadline date is the visual.

**Voice note:** This ad does not catastrophize. "You're closer to needing it now" is accurate (fewer days remaining) and factual without alarm-toning. The buyer knows they're interested — the retargeting ad's only job is to remove the "I'll come back to this later" loophole. Naming the exact product they viewed and the exact deadline is the most specific version of that removal.

## Imagery Direction Across All Ads

One paragraph: Ad imagery should show ordinary humans in ordinary work contexts — the HR manager at a laptop, the small restaurant owner with paperwork, the person on a phone call in an office. No AI imagery (no circuit boards, no neural network diagrams, no glowing brains). No legal symbolism (no gavels, no scales of justice, no courtrooms). No staged group photos of diverse professionals around a conference table. The buyer is usually alone when they find this product. The image should feel like their world.

For deadline-anchored ads (Colorado), a simple typographic treatment — deadline date large, white text on Deadline Amber — is stronger than any photograph. The date is the content.
