# Keyword Strategy — Contestant 4

## Research Methodology

Queries below were identified through:
1. Live WebSearch on "Colorado SB 24-205 AI compliance template small business 2026" — identified competitor positioning and SERP landscape
2. Live WebSearch on buyer intent patterns: "'ai compliance' 'small business' 'how do I' OR 'where do I start' OR 'do I need'"
3. Live WebSearch on autocomplete/PAA patterns: "'ai compliance' 'colorado ai' 'nyc bias audit' 'illinois ai hiring' small business 2026"
4. First Page Sage CTR benchmarks to understand click value by position
5. pathopt.com buyer pain points to identify question-format queries
6. GSC data from brief: existing high-performer queries and position data

All queries are buyer-intent (problem-aware, deadline-aware, or action-ready) — not researcher-intent (framework, overview, "what is").

## SERP Structure Note (AI Overviews)

Live research finding: Google Search results for "Colorado AI compliance" and related terms show Google AI Overviews consuming general definitional answers ("what is SB24-205") in-SERP, which explains the 0.11% CTR at position 4.16 for the EEOC post. The EEOC post is answering a question ("what happened to federal AI guidance?") that Google now answers in an AI Overview box — so organic CTR collapses.

The mitigation strategy: target queries where the buyer needs a specific artifact (a template, a compliance package, a document) rather than an explanation. Google AI Overviews answer "what is" questions but do not yet replace "where to get" queries. The high-intent queries in this list — "colorado ai law compliance documents," "illinois hb3773 compliance template," "nyc aedt compliance" — are artifact queries, not explanation queries. They should be more resistant to AI Overview cannibalization.

Source: [First Page Sage CTR by position report](https://firstpagesage.com/reports/google-click-through-rates-ctrs-by-ranking-position/) — AI Overviews appear on ~31% of SERPs but organic CTRs show resilience (not collapsing the way paid ads are). The issue is specifically where the query intent is answerable in a summary — pure informational queries.
