# Round 2 Choice — Contestant 4

## Path: B-lean (Commit Harder)

## Reasoning

My Round 1 frame — single structural bet, deadline date and state law name in every above-the-fold surface — is correct and more distinctive than anything in the field summary. The scope expansion doesn't invalidate the frame; it asks me to extend it into dimensions I didn't touch (visual language, brand voice, information architecture, ad creative). The Proxy also identified the one real structural gap: the deadline-anchored frame works for Colorado's future deadline but fails for laws already in force — NYC, Illinois, Texas. The sibling frame the Proxy gestured toward ("already non-compliant" vs. "deadline approaching") is the most important thing to add in Round 2. It makes the single bet cover the whole inventory instead of just one product. The field converged on the hub page, the H1 visibility, and the CTA rewrite — I don't need to change my conceptual direction to incorporate those; they're already in my work. What I need to do is stretch the frame: two modes (deadline-approaching / already-exposed), a sharp new marketing voice (not the journalist-explainer voice, not a generic SaaS voice — something that sounds like a specialist who knows exactly what you owe), and the visual and IA dimensions that make the urgency register visually before any copy is read.

## What I'll Do

Build the two-mode urgency frame: "Deadline Approaching" (Colorado, future effective laws) and "Already Exposed" (NYC, Illinois, Texas — laws in force with active enforcement). Write a new marketing voice spec that sounds like a compliance specialist handing you a checklist — short, authoritative, product-forward — not the explainer voice of the blog. Design the visual direction around urgency signals: the palette, the type, the hero pattern, the product card should visually read "this is a store with something you need" not "this is a resource hub." Rewrite all the marketing-surface copy in the new voice. Write the ad creative with deadline/exposure framing per state. Keep the integrity callouts (Colorado $20K provenance, statute citations) intact — voice change does not relax sourcing discipline.
