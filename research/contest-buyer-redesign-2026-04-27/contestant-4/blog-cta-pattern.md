# Blog CTA Pattern — Contestant 4 (v2)

*(Round 1 version archived as `blog-cta-pattern-v1.md`. This version rewrites all CTA copy in the new marketing voice. The three-placement structure and post-tier system carry forward unchanged.)*

---

## The Problem (unchanged from v1)

One product link per 1,500-word post. No standalone urgency copy. The buyer reads about a $70,000/violation penalty and the next sentence continues explaining the statute. The CTA pattern closes that gap.

Three placements per law-specific post. Two inline (applicability moment + penalty moment). One standalone block (action moment). The structure is v1. The copy is new.

---

## Voice Standard for Blog CTAs

Blog body copy stays in the Pragmatic Realist / Precise Credentialist voice per BLOG-STYLE-GUIDE.md. The CTA blocks shift to the new marketing voice — short, declarative, offer-shaped. The contrast is intentional: the article informs, the CTA sells. A reader who has read 1,400 words of statute explanation should meet a CTA that feels like a different register — cleaner, faster, more direct.

The two urgency modes (Deadline Approaching / Already Exposed) apply to CTA copy just as they do to product pages.

---

## Placement 1: Early Inline CTA

**When:** Within the first H2 section or after the opening applicability confirmation (~300–400 words in).

**Template:**
```
[LAW] applies to you. The [N]-document compliance package is here — $[PRICE], instant download.
[Product link text: Get the [STATE] [LAW SHORT NAME] Documents →]
```

**Colorado example (Deadline Approaching):**
> SB 24-205 applies to your business. The [8-document Colorado compliance package](/products/colorado-sb24-205) is here — $449, built from C.R.S. § 6-1-1701 et seq. June 30, 2026 deadline.

**Illinois example (Already Exposed):**
> HB3773 applies to you. The [5-document Illinois compliance package](/products/illinois-hb3773) is here — $249, built from 775 ILCS 5/2-102(L). The law is in effect now.

**NYC example (Already Exposed):**
> Local Law 144 covers your business. The [7-document NYC compliance package](/products/nyc-local-law-144) is here — $399, built from NYC Admin. Code § 20-870. DCWP investigations are increasing.

**Voice note:** v1 Placement 1 copy was: "If SB 24-205 applies to your business — meaning you deploy AI that substantially influences consequential decisions about Colorado residents — the Colorado SB 24-205 compliance package gives you all 8 documents required of deployers, built from the enacted statute text at leg.colorado.gov." That's 44 words. v2 is 18 words. The conditional clause ("if...meaning...") is removed — the reader just confirmed applicability in the preceding paragraphs. The CTA assumes they're in and sells directly.

## Placement 2: Penalty Section Inline CTA (Mandatory)

**When:** Immediately after the penalty figures in the enforcement/penalties section. Every law-specific post that discusses penalties must have this placement.

**Template:**
```
[PRODUCT LINK] — $[PRICE]. [Document count] documents. [Deadline or status]. Download today.
```

**Colorado example (Deadline Approaching):**
> Penalties of up to $20,000 per violation — each affected consumer a potential separate count. [The Colorado compliance package](/products/colorado-sb24-205) is $449. 8 documents. June 30, 2026 deadline. Download today.

**Illinois example (Already Exposed):**
> Penalties up to $70,000 per violation for repeat offenses ([775 ILCS 5/8A-104](https://www.ilga.gov/legislation/ilcs/documents/077500050K8A-104.htm)). [The Illinois compliance package](/products/illinois-hb3773) is $249. 5 documents. The law is in effect now. Download today.

**NYC example (Already Exposed):**
> $500–$1,500 per day per ongoing violation (NYC Admin. Code § 20-871(d)). [The NYC compliance package](/products/nyc-local-law-144) is $399. 7 documents. DCWP enforcement is active. Download today.

**Voice note:** v1 Placement 2 was: "Penalties of up to $20,000 per violation — each affected consumer potentially a separate count — make documentation the most cost-effective first step. The Colorado SB 24-205 compliance package ($449) includes all 8 documents required of deployers, built from [C.R.S. § 6-1-1701 et seq.] — the same statute the AG enforces from." That's 56 words. v2 is 22 words. The framing sentence ("make documentation the most cost-effective first step") is cut — the buyer just read the penalty. They don't need the logical bridge. The CTA is price, count, deadline, action.

**When:** After the final H2, before the `---` divider. Standalone section.

**Template:**

**Voice note:** v1 Placement 3 was 40–60 words per block, including a sentence explaining what the package covers. v2 compresses to a single declarative sentence (status + count + price + source + speed) followed by two links. The explanation belongs in the product page — the blog CTA's job is to move the buyer there, not to re-explain the product.

## Two-Mode CTA Application Guide

**Deadline Approaching posts** (Colorado, any law with future effective date):
- Placement 1: "The deadline is [DATE]." appears in inline CTA
- Placement 2: penalty sentence ends with "[DATE] deadline. Download today."
- Placement 3 block header: "[LAW]. Get the Documents." Body leads with date.

**Already Exposed posts** (Illinois, NYC, Texas, CA — laws in force):
- Placement 1: "The law is in effect now." replaces date reference
- Placement 2: penalty sentence ends with "The law is in effect now. Download today."
- Placement 3 block header: same structure. Body leads with "In effect now."

No hybrid — each post belongs to one mode. The EEOC post (multi-law, multi-state) uses the generic multi-state variant.

## Implementation Notes (unchanged from v1, kept for reference)

**MDX compatibility:** All CTA copy uses standard markdown link syntax `[text](url)` and `##` headers. No HTML, no custom shortcodes. `→` (Unicode right arrow) renders in all supported markdown. Verified against BLOG-STYLE-GUIDE.md supported markdown list.

**Priority order for implementation:**
1. `colorado-sb-24-205-ai-law-what-businesses-need-to-know.mdx` — 855 impressions, 0 clicks
2. `eeoc-ai-guidance-removed-federal-vacuum-2026.mdx` — 1,833 impressions at position 4.16
3. `illinois-hb3773-ai-employment-law-what-employers-need.mdx` — in effect now
4. `ai-compliance-cost-small-business-2026.mdx` — buyer-intent query, site at position 3
5. Remaining Tier 1 law-specific posts by GSC impressions

**What not to do** (unchanged from v1): No newsletter capture as primary CTA. No "schedule a consultation." No "speak with an expert." No "get started in minutes" without specifying what. No CTA in the opening hook before applicability is confirmed.
