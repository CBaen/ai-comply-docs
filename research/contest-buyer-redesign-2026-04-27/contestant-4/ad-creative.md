# Ad Creative — Contestant 4

## Voice Note

All ad copy uses the new marketing voice (voice-spec.md): short, declarative, offer-shaped, specific to law and deadline. No "discover," no abstract compliance-noun piles. The two modes (Deadline Approaching / Already Exposed) apply per law.

## Google Ads — 3 RSAs

### RSA 1: Colorado SB 24-205 (Deadline Approaching mode)

**Target query:** `colorado sb 24-205 compliance documents`

**Headlines (3, each ≤30 chars):**
1. `Colorado AI Deadline: June 30`
2. `SB 24-205 — 8 Documents Ready`
3. `$449. Built From Statute. Now.`

**Descriptions (2, each ≤90 chars):**
1. `Colorado's AI law takes effect June 30, 2026. Get the 8 documents it requires — $449, instant download.`
2. `Impact assessments, risk policy, consumer notices. Built from C.R.S. § 6-1-1701. Not AI-generated summaries.`

**Display URL path:** `aicompliancedocuments.com/colorado-sb24-205`

**Rationale:** Headline 1 leads with the date — the buyer's primary urgency. Headline 2 delivers the offer (8 documents, ready now). Headline 3 delivers price and speed. Description 1 names three of the specific documents (buyers who know what they need will scan for these terms). Description 2 names the citation and the differentiator ("not AI-generated summaries") — addressing the trust objection this niche has about AI-generated legal content.

**Note on headline 1:** "Colorado AI Deadline: June 30" is 28 characters — within Google's 30-char limit. If the law delays or changes, this headline must be updated before it ships. The date is from [leg.colorado.gov/bills/sb24-205](https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/sb24-205) + SB25B-004 (verified Round 1).

Description 2 lists the three primary obligations (audit, notices, disclosure) and maps to the statute — both the document count and the citation.

**NYC penalty figures:** $500 first violation; $500–$1,500/day per subsequent violation — per NYC Admin. Code § 20-871(d), verified via NY State Comptroller audit and DLA Piper (Round 1 research).

## Reddit Native Ad

**Platform:** r/smallbusiness or r/Compliance
**Format:** Native (text-forward, not display banner)

**Title (≤60 chars):**
`Does Colorado's AI Law Apply to Your Business?`

**Body (≤500 chars):**
```
Colorado's AI law (SB 24-205) takes effect June 30, 2026. If your business uses AI in hiring, insurance, lending, housing, or healthcare decisions — and it affects Colorado residents — you're a "deployer" under this law. That means impact assessments, a risk management policy, and consumer notices before the deadline.

The compliance package is 8 documents, built from the enacted statute (not a law firm's summary). $449, instant download.

Not sure if it applies? Check which state laws apply to you: aicompliancedocuments.com/compliance-deadline-by-state
```

**Image direction (Unsplash search query):**
`"small business owner laptop serious office"`

A real-looking person at a desk, slightly stressed, working — not a posed stock photo. No AI imagery, no corporate setting. The image should make an r/smallbusiness member think "that's someone like me, dealing with this problem."

**Rationale:** Reddit r/smallbusiness is the right venue because the buyer is exactly the person who reads those threads — an owner or operator of a SMB who has discovered a compliance problem and doesn't know where to start. Native-feel means: no exclamation marks, no promotional language, no fabricated urgency. The framing is informational ("does this apply to you?") and then delivers an offer ("here's the package"). The body walks through the applicability check — answering the scope confusion objection before the buyer even clicks. The closing link to `/compliance-deadline-by-state` captures buyers who aren't sure Colorado applies to them and don't want to waste $449 finding out.

**What makes this Reddit-native and not display-ad:**
- Opens with a question, not a claim
- No exclamation marks
- No "best," "amazing," "you'll love it"
- Explicitly names the law, the deadline, and the document count (specificity reads as credibility on Reddit)
- Ends with a low-commitment secondary path (the deadline check page)

## Retargeting Concept

**Target:** Someone who visited `/products/colorado-sb24-205` and bounced without purchasing. Retargeting served on Google Display Network or Meta.

**Headline:**
`Colorado's Deadline Is Still June 30`

**Description:**
`You looked at the SB 24-205 compliance package. The deadline didn't move. 8 documents, $449. Download today.`

**CTA button:**
`Get Your Colorado Documents`

**Visual direction:**
Clean white card, Midnight Navy text, Deadline Amber accent on "June 30." No imagery — the card is copy-forward. The design mirrors the product card from the site (familiar visual language for someone who already visited).

**Rationale:** A retargeted buyer has already done the research. They know the law, they saw the product, they left without buying. The retargeting ad doesn't need to sell the concept — it removes the obstacle. "You looked. The deadline didn't move. The price is the same." That's the only message needed. No new information, no new benefits. Just deadline reinforcement and a direct path back to purchase.

**Frequency cap:** 5 impressions/person/7 days. Compliance urgency is real, but over-retargeting a compliance-anxious buyer creates negative brand association. 5 is enough to register without creating avoidance.

## Imagery Direction Across All Ads

The visual register for all ad creative: **real people, real workplaces, no compliance clichés.**

Specific rules:
- No scales of justice, no gavels, no courthouse imagery
- No AI-visualization imagery (neural networks, glowing nodes, data streams)
- No corporate headshots or "diverse team celebrating" stock imagery
- Real-looking people: a business owner at a laptop, an HR manager with documents, a healthcare administrator at a screen
- Color: use Midnight Navy as overlay on images to create brand consistency across placements
- For retargeting ads: text-forward cards (no background image) — familiar to the product page visual the buyer already saw
