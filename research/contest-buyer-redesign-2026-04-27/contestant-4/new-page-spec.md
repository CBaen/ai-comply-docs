# New Page Spec — Contestant 4

## Page: `/compliance-deadline-by-state`

**Route:** `/compliance-deadline-by-state`

**Type:** Static page (no dynamic params needed — same structure as homepage sections)

**Why this page is necessary:**

Two problems in the current site require a dedicated landing page:
1. The #1 buyer objection is scope confusion: "Do I even need to worry about this?" Buyers need a fast, state-organized answer before they can navigate to a product.
2. Multiple blog posts and homepage CTAs need a destination for buyers who don't know which state's law applies to them. Currently the only destination is `/products` (the full catalog, unfiltered) — which is catalog browsing, not buyer guidance.

This page serves three specific traffic sources: (a) buyers arriving from the sub-CTA "Not sure which law applies to you? Start here →" on the homepage, (b) buyers arriving from the blog CTA "See all state compliance packages and deadlines," and (c) organic buyers searching "which state AI laws apply to my business" or "AI compliance deadline by state."

### Section 4: SEO-Targeted FAQ (4 questions)

```
Q: Does my business need to comply with state AI laws if we're headquartered in a different state?

A: Yes. Colorado SB 24-205, Illinois HB3773, and most other state AI laws apply based on where your employees, applicants, or consumers are located — not where your business is incorporated or headquartered. A Texas company with Colorado employees faces the same Colorado SB 24-205 obligations as a Colorado-headquartered company.

Q: Do I need to comply with the law if the AI is built into my vendor's software?

A: In most cases, yes. Under Colorado SB 24-205 and Illinois HB3773, compliance obligations fall on the deployer — the business using the AI system — not the vendor who built it. Your vendor's compliance does not substitute for yours. You may have disclosure and documentation obligations regardless of whether the vendor also complies.

Q: What if the law is still being implemented or rulemaking isn't complete?

A: The obligation is based on the enacted statute, not implementing rules. Colorado's AG has stated it can bring enforcement actions before completing rulemaking. Illinois HB3773 is in effect despite IDHR's implementing rules being pending. Waiting for final implementing rules to begin compliance creates legal exposure.

Q: What exactly do I need to buy to be compliant?

A: It depends on the specific law and your role under it. Each product page lists the exact documents included and maps them to specific statutory requirements. Start with the state where your employees or consumers are — then check the product page to confirm the document list matches your compliance obligation.
```
