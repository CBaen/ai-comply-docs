# Loop 2 — What Changed, What Held

The Proxy coach identified a single structural gap: the two-mode frame (Deadline Approaching / Already Exposed) was named but not mechanized — the spec didn't answer how a product page knows which mode to render, or how a buyer who arrived on the Colorado page learns they also have Illinois exposure. Loop 2 closed both gaps without altering the frame itself. Three additions: (1) a flip-logic table in `product-page-template.md` mapping `status === "effective-soon"` vs. `status === "in-effect"` to every affected UI element — badge color, H1 text, sidebar label, penalty section header, meta title — with a manual one-field update in `regulations.ts` as the trigger (deliberate, not automated, to avoid flash-of-wrong-mode on render); (2) an `AlsoExposedStrip` component spec for Deadline Approaching product pages, built on a `CROSS_STATE_EXPOSURE: Record<string, string[]>` mapping filtered live by `status === "in-effect"`, positioned after the penalty section, styled in Enforcement Red — gives the multi-state buyer who arrived via SERP the cross-mode signal they would have gotten from the homepage urgency panel if they'd gone there; (3) full July 1, 2026 state-of-the-site spec across `ia-proposal.md` and `homepage-rewrite.md` — urgency panel goes all-red, homepage Section 2 loses its "Deadline Approaching" subsection header, meta description becomes present-tense all-exposed language (one manual update to `page.tsx`), and the `/compliance-deadline-by-state` page title shifts from "Deadlines" to "Laws in Effect." What was held: the two-mode frame and its Deadline Amber / Enforcement Red visual system, all Round 2 voice spec, visual direction, information architecture, ad creative, and all penalty citations with their integrity flags intact.
