# Round 1 Complete — Contestant 4

## Concept Summary

The redesign reframes aicompliancedocuments.com from a compliance catalog (researcher bait) into a deadline-anchored emergency response (buyer bait). The central insight: the site's traffic problem is a mismatch problem, not a volume problem. 10,513 impressions exist; buyers are in the audience; the site is not reading as "this is where you go when you have a deadline" because no current element — not the title, not the H1, not the meta description, not the hero — names a specific state law or specific deadline date. The redesign puts Colorado SB 24-205 (June 30, 2026), Illinois HB3773 (in effect now), and NYC Local Law 144 (enforcement escalating) in the title tag, meta description, H1, and urgency bar before the buyer reads anything else. The blog CTA pattern closes the conversion leak by adding three CTA placements per post — an early inline (applicability moment), a mandatory penalty-section inline (consequence moment), and a standalone block CTA — instead of the current one prose-embedded link per 1,500 words. The `/compliance-deadline-by-state` new page gives scope-confused buyers a self-qualification pathway before they're ready to navigate the product catalog.

All penalty figures, effective dates, and statute citations in this submission were verified against primary sources or against the site's own verified blog content during this session. The Colorado $20,000/violation figure is sourced from [aicerts.ai](https://www.aicerts.ai/news/colorado-ai-law-key-duties-penalties-and-2026-deadline/) and the site's own verified blog, derived from the Colorado Consumer Protection Act enforcement mechanism via SB24-205 — and is flagged for developer verification against the current C.R.S. § 6-1-112 before publication.
