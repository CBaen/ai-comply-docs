# Keyword Strategy — Contestant 1

## Strategy Frame

The site currently ranks for researcher queries ("ai governance standards," "ai compliance framework"). The keyword strategy must shift targeting to buyer-intent queries — problem-aware searches by business owners who know they have a deadline and are looking for a solution, not an explanation.

**Research basis:** Live search results (2026-04-27) show that the top-ranking competitors for these queries are law firms (Ogletree, Seyfarth), enterprise compliance platforms (TrustArc, LogicGate), and general guides. None of them sell a $449 instant-download template package. The gap is real: buyers who want to avoid $400/hour attorney fees AND don't need an enterprise platform have nowhere to go except here. The keyword strategy makes that gap visible.

## What the Site Does NOT Currently Target (Opportunity Gap)

The current homepage targets "ai governance framework template" (a researcher query) and "ai compliance for small business" (a broad awareness query). Neither maps to a purchase-intent buyer in a specific state.

The site does NOT currently appear in search results for:
- "texas traiga compliance template" (TXAIMS owns this)
- "nyc bias audit template" (nycbiasaudit.com + Warden AI own this)
- "illinois ai hiring notice template" (law firm content dominates)

Each of these is a winnable product-page query if the page copy is restructured to match buyer intent — not catalog language.

The leading indicator of success is not blog impressions — it is product page impressions at buyer-intent queries. The current 350-impression / 1-click performance on the Colorado product page is the baseline to beat.
