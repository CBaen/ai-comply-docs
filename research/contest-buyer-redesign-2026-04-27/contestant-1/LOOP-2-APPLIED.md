# Loop 2 Applied — Contestant 1

Three changes, all in the visual/copy layer. None require rethinking the thesis.

**What changed:** The eyebrow now names all four states ("TEXAS · ILLINOIS · NYC · COLORADO — AI LAWS ARE IN EFFECT") at `text-sm` instead of a generic "AI LAWS ARE IN EFFECT" at `text-xs` — the Proxy was right that a panicked Texas buyer searching at 11pm reads the eyebrow before the H1 and needs to see their state name before they've done any work. The H1 changed one verb: "passed" (past tense, legislative history) to "is in effect" (present tense, current enforcement reality) — same eleven-word structure, but the temporal frame now matches what the buyer actually needs to know. The deadline pills became a "State Urgency Band" — larger (`text-sm font-bold px-4 py-2 rounded-md` vs `text-xs px-3 py-1 rounded-full`), reordered (in-effect states first: Texas, Illinois, NYC — then Colorado's future deadline), all one color (Ember Red — the text carries the temporal distinction; color-coding two states differently requires decoding time a panic-state buyer doesn't have). The voice-spec gained a paragraph distinguishing calm-authority at two arrival altitudes: organized buyer vs. panicked buyer. The voice doesn't change between them — but the visual layer's job is to catch the panicked buyer before the voice can steady them.

**What I held:** The Proxy asked whether the H1 is stateless and whether a state name should appear in it. I considered it and held the generic H1. The eyebrow now does the state-specific arrival work — naming Texas, Illinois, NYC, Colorado in order. Putting a state name inside the H1 would require choosing one state (which state leads? Colorado because of the deadline? Texas because of the penalty?) or listing all four (which makes the H1 unwieldy). The eyebrow-to-H1 layering is the right structure: eyebrow catches by naming states, H1 closes by naming the offer. The panicked Texas buyer reads TEXAS in the eyebrow's first word, reads the H1 as the resolution. That sequencing works without collapsing the H1 into a list.
