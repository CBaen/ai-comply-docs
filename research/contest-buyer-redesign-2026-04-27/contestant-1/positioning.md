# Positioning Statement — Contestant 1

**aicompliancedocuments.com** serves small and mid-size business owners who use AI in hiring, HR, lending, insurance, or consumer-facing decisions, and who have just discovered — from counsel, a vendor audit, a regulatory notice, or a Google search at 11pm — that they are legally exposed under Colorado SB 24-205, Illinois HB3773, NYC Local Law 144, Texas TRAIGA, or California ADMT rules. These owners have no compliance officer, no time to read 40-page statutes, and no budget for law firm drafting hours. They need one thing: a professionally prepared set of documents that demonstrate they took the law seriously, built against what the statute actually says, available in ten minutes for a fraction of what outside counsel charges. The site must stop reading like a legal research hub and start reading like the fastest path from exposure to documented.

---

## The Diagnostic Frame

The site currently positions itself as a catalog of AI compliance templates for researchers. The positioning pivot is not about adding urgency language — it is about **naming the buyer's exact situation in the first five words of every page** so that a business owner who just got a letter from their auditor knows within three seconds whether they are in the right place.

**Old frame:** "Templates for every state AI law" — speaks to comprehensiveness, appeals to researchers, feels like a reference library.

**New frame:** "You have a compliance deadline. Here is the documentation." — speaks to urgency, names the buyer's situation, feels like a solution.

The buyer is not searching for "AI governance standards." They are searching for "do I need to comply with Colorado AI law" or "Illinois AI hiring law compliance documents" or "NYC bias audit template." The site should answer those queries on the product page, not just the blog.
