## The Perspective Shift

Here is the question I want you to sit with: your strategy says pivot AWAY from informational queries, because AI Overviews consume them. You argue that transactional queries — "Illinois HB3773 compliance template" — are what to target instead. That is a sound strategic argument. But look at what you actually have: 1,833 impressions/month arriving at the EEOC blog post at position 4.16. Those 1,833 people are already on the site — or one click away from it. Some percentage of them are the HR director who read the EEOC news and thought "wait, does this apply to us?" They didn't arrive via a transactional query; they arrived via an informational one. But they may have transactional intent underneath that informational surface.

Your strategy as written serves the buyers you don't yet have (people searching transactionally) but doesn't say anything about the buyers who are already arriving (people searching informationally who might convert). Those aren't two different populations — they may be the same person at two different moments in a 72-hour buying window. Ask yourself: does the Zero-Click Pivot abandon the EEOC blog visitor, or does it serve them? What would your strategy look like if you had to do both — stop chasing informational rankings you can't out-click, AND convert the informational traffic that already arrives?
