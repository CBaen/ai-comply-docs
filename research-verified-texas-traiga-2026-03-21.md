# Verified Research — Texas TRAIGA (HB 149) — March 21, 2026

## Status: DUAL-VERIFIED

**Bill:** HB 149 (NOT HB 1709 — that version died in committee)
**Name:** Texas Responsible Artificial Intelligence Governance Act
**Signed:** June 22, 2025 by Gov. Abbott
**Effective:** January 1, 2026
**Author:** Rep. Giovanni Capriglione
**Passed:** House 146-3, Senate 31-0

## Key Statute Facts
- AG exclusive enforcement, no private right of action (Sec. 552.101)
- Rebuttable presumption defendant used reasonable care (Sec. 552.105(c))
- Safe harbor: substantial compliance with NIST Gen AI Profile (AI 600-1) or equivalent (Sec. 552.105(e))
- 60-day cure period before enforcement action (Sec. 552.104)
- Penalties: $10K-$12K curable, $80K-$200K uncurable, $2K-$40K/day continuing (Sec. 552.105(a))
- Intent required for discrimination — disparate impact alone insufficient (Sec. 552.056(c))
- 36-month regulatory sandbox with legal immunity (Sec. 553.051, 553.053)
- AG complaint portal required by September 1, 2026 (Sec. 552.102)
- No small-business exemption, no size/revenue threshold
- Federally insured financial institutions get safe harbor under existing banking laws (Sec. 552.056(e))
- Does NOT use "high-risk" classification — applies to any AI system
- Texas preempts local governments from passing AI ordinances (Sec. 552.003)
- Developer/deployer distinction exists but obligations not as differentiated as Colorado
