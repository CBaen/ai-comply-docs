## Verified Statute Facts (from enrolled bill PDF)

### Definitions
- **High-risk AI system** (§ 6-1-1701(9)(a)): AI that makes or is a substantial factor in making a consequential decision. Exclusions in (9)(b) for narrow procedural tasks, pattern detection with human review, anti-fraud, cybersecurity, etc.
- **Consequential decision** (§ 6-1-1701(3)): Material legal or significant effect in 8 domains: (a) education, (b) employment, (c) financial/lending, (d) government services, (e) healthcare, (f) housing, (g) insurance, (h) legal services.

### Developer Obligations (§ 6-1-1702)
- Use reasonable care to protect from algorithmic discrimination (1)
- Provide deployers: harmful use statement, training data docs, evaluation docs, additional docs needed (2)(a-d)
- Provide impact assessment documentation (model cards, dataset cards) (3)
- Publish public statement on website re: system types and risk management (4)
- Disclose discrimination risks to AG + known deployers within 90 days (5)
- Trade secret protections preserved (6)
- AG can request documentation within 90 days; not subject to CORA (7)

### Deployer Obligations (§ 6-1-1703)
- Use reasonable care (1)
- Risk management policy and program — must consider NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001, size, complexity (2)
- Impact assessment at initial deployment, annually, and within 90 days of substantial modification (3)(a-b)
- Retain impact assessments 3+ years (3)(f)
- Annual review of deployed systems (3)(g)
- Consumer notification BEFORE deployment: purpose, nature of decision, contact info, description, opt-out rights (4)(a)
- After adverse decision: principal reasons, data correction opportunity, appeal with human review if feasible (4)(b)
- Public website statement (5)
- Disclose discrimination to AG within 90 days (7)

### Size Exemption (§ 6-1-1703(6))
- Deployers with <50 FTE who don't use own data to train, use system for intended purposes, and share developer's impact assessment are EXEMPT from risk management policy, impact assessment, and public statement requirements.
- NOT exempt from consumer notification or other obligations.
- No revenue threshold exists.

### Exemptions
- **Insurance**: Full compliance if subject to § 10-3-1104.9 (§ 6-1-1705(7))
- **Banks/credit unions**: Full compliance if examined by state/federal prudential regulator under published AI guidance (§ 6-1-1705(8))

### Affirmative Defense (§ 6-1-1706(3))
Requires BOTH:
(a) Discovered and cured violation through: feedback, adversarial testing/red teaming, or internal review
AND
(b) Compliance with: NIST AI RMF + ISO/IEC 42001, OR equivalent framework, OR AG-designated framework
Burden is on the defendant. Applies only to AG enforcement actions.

### Enforcement
- AG has exclusive authority (§ 6-1-1706(1))
- Violations = deceptive trade practice under CCPA (§ 6-1-1706(2))
- No private right of action (§ 6-1-1706(6))
- Penalties: Up to $20,000/violation (C.R.S. § 6-1-112(1)(a)), $50,000 for age 60+ (§ 6-1-112(1)(c))
- Note: Penalty amounts are from the Colorado Consumer Protection Act, not SB 24-205 itself

### AG Rulemaking Status (as of March 20, 2026)
- Formal notice-and-comment rulemaking has NOT YET BEGUN
- Pre-rulemaking input window is closed
- No enforcement guidance published
- AG website: coag.gov/ai/

## Verified Micro-Facts (for "Did You Know?" sidebar)

1. Violations treated as deceptive trade practices under CCPA — existing penalty structures apply, not bespoke fines. (Source: Colorado General Assembly)
2. AG has exclusive enforcement — no private right of action. (Source: Colorado General Assembly)
3. Affirmative defense for following NIST AI RMF — direct statutory safe harbor. (Source: Colorado General Assembly)
4. Insurers exempt if already subject to Colorado algorithm/predictive model laws. (Source: Colorado General Assembly)
5. Banks/credit unions exempt if under state/federal prudential regulator AI guidance. (Source: Colorado General Assembly)
6. Developers must disclose discrimination risks to AG + deployers within 90 days. (Source: Colorado General Assembly)
7. Federal EO 14110 on AI was rescinded Jan 20, 2025 — makes state laws like SB 24-205 more significant. (Source: NIST)
8. NIST AI RMF has 12 published crosswalks to EU, Japan, Korea, Singapore, ISO standards. (Source: NIST AIRC)
9. NIST crosswalk shows Colorado's framework shares DNA with EU AI Act. (Source: NIST AIRC)
10. NIST AI RMF 1.0 is currently being revised — the affirmative defense framework is a moving target. (Source: NIST AIRC)
